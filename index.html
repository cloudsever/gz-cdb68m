What are the strengths and weaknesses of each team's pitching rotation?

Pitching will play a pivotal role in Game 3. Let's take a closer look at each team's rotation:

New York Yankees

Strengths:

  • Ace Performance: Gerrit Cole has been lights out this postseason, consistently delivering quality starts.
  • Bullpen Depth: The Yankees boast a strong bullpen with several reliable options like Clay Holmes and Michael King.

Weaknesses:

  • Inconsistency: Other starters have struggled with command at times, which could be exploited by a potent Dodgers lineup.
  • Injury Concerns: Some key pitchers have battled injuries throughout the season, raising questions about their effectiveness.

Los Angeles Dodgers

Strengths:

  • Star Power: Clayton Kershaw remains one of the best pitchers in baseball history, capable of dominating any lineup.
  • Versatile Options: The Dodgers can mix and match pitchers effectively based on matchups.

Weaknesses:

  • Reliance on Starters: If Kershaw or another starter falters early, it could put pressure on an otherwise solid bullpen.
  • Inexperience: Some younger pitchers may lack experience in high-pressure situations like the World Series.

How does home-field advantage play a role in the dynamics of Game 3?

Statistics show that home teams generally win more often during playoffs; thus, this factor cannot be understated as we head into Game 3.
